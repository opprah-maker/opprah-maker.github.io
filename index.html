Featured : Individual Project Dissertation

Reforming the UK Energy Market for Sustainable Development

Integrating renewables, fair pricing, and demand-side response. MATLAB-based hybrid renewable energy system (HRES) scenario modelling comparing 2030, 2040, and 2050 decarbonisation pathways for the UK electricity grid. Key findings: coordinated HRES deployment plus demand-side flexibility can cut carbon emissions by ~70 percent by 2050 while keeping consumer prices within +/-5 percent of baseline.

Scenario Comparison : 2030, 2040, 2050

Side-by-side comparison of three decarbonisation pathways across carbon emissions, renewable generation share, wholesale price, and grid stability metrics. The coordinated HRES pathway (with demand-side response) outperforms both the business-as-usual and renewables-only scenarios.

About this Portfolio

Engineering Reports

Five independent submissions prepared for an integrated aerospace and electrical engineering programme. Each report contains the full problem formulation, methodology, results, and discussion, and is supported by extracted figures, datasets, and source code in the linked repository.

Toolchain

  • ANSYS Fluent and CFX for CFD and FSI
  • MATLAB for conceptual sizing, FDM, and energy modelling
  • Python for automation and figure generation
  • LaTeX and aspose-words for report preparation

Topic Coverage

  • Aerodynamics : wind-tunnel data, polar plots, Cp distributions

  • CFD : turbulence modelling, mesh convergence, validation
  • Gas turbine inlets : bellmouth profile optimisation
  • Energy systems : HRES scenario modelling, demand-side response
  • Numerical methods : finite difference schemes, verification
